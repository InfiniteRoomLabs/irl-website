## The problem

Consultants routinely recommend infrastructure they have never operated. I wanted every recommendation backed by the experience of running it: the upgrade that breaks, the alert that fires at 3am for nothing.

## The system

A k3s Kubernetes cluster running a real service fleet (source control, identity, databases, monitoring, storage) under the same rules a client environment would get.

- Every service deploys through Helm charts driven by Ansible playbooks. No `kubectl apply` from a laptop. The repo is the environment.
- Secrets have one source of truth: a password manager feeds a sync pipeline that renders them into both Ansible Vault and Kubernetes Secrets, with rotation-policy checks on a schedule. Nothing is pasted, and nothing lives in two places.
- PostgreSQL runs under CloudNativePG, so replicas and backups are managed as code, upgrades included.
- Prometheus and Loki feed Grafana dashboards and alerts that watch the golden signals.
- Internal services resolve through a DNS zone generated from the same service inventory that drives deployment.

## Why it matters

None of this is exotic. That is the point. The discipline is the product: a lab run with production habits. Knowing the difference between a lab and production is exactly what you hire for.
